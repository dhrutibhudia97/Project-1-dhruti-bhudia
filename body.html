Why is exercise so good for us?

Exercise has many well known health benefits such as: lowering blood pressure, reducing body fat and strengthening muscles and our heart. But exercise does just benefit us physically, but mentally too. When we exercise, our body releases hormones called endorphins. These endorphins can similar to pain killers as well as making us feel happy.

Exercising on a regular basis has been proven to:

  • Helps combat the feeling of anxiety and depression
  • Boosts self-esteem
  • Improves our quality of sleep
  • Reduces stress

Here are some ways to ease your way into being more active:

  • Start with small goals like going for evening walks or joining a local class that you like.
  • Go easy on yourself, as long as you are doing something extra compared to your normal routine you are taking a step in the right direction.
  • Even you daily chores can count e.g. cleaning the house or putting the clothes out to dry. Its easier than you think to be active.
  • Use it as a way to clear your head, e.g. exercising after you finish work to stop you from thinking about work and allowing you to enjoy your evening.
  • Reward yourself! Completed all your weekly goals? Treat yourself, we are all human after all!

The importance of sleep

Not only do we need enough sleep (around 7-9 hours a night if you’re an adult) but we need good quality sleep. Sleep is essential as it allows our minds and bodies to recharge and it allows our brain to function properly. Our sleep cycle is regulated by a 24 hour cycle called the circadian rhythm. Light has an effect on this, in the evening when the natural light disappears, our body release hormones that make us drowsy. The opposite occurs when the sun rises, our body releases hormones that make us feel more alert.

There are small ways in which we can improve our habits to get a better quality sleep:

  • Having a realistic time to be in bed for all days of the week.
  • Have light blocking curtains or an eye mask.
  • Not looking at screens at least 30 minutes before your bedtime.
  • Not having caffeine 6 hours before your bedtime.
  • Exercise during the day to help unwind in the evening and tire your body for sleep

How yoga can effect our wellbeing

There is more to yoga than just deep breath with your eyes closed and crossed legs. Yoga can help with many things from focussing our attention, improving our posture to improving our breathing though exercise. Yoga can be a stress relief that impacts positively on our mental health.

It has been proven that yoga can:
  • Improve sleep quality
  • Relieve muscle pain
  • Help with weight loss
  • Improve flexibility
  • Reduce depression and anxiety
